About Gavi

Gavi, the Vaccine Alliance is a global public–private partnership created in 2000 to improve access to new and underused vaccines for children living in the world’s poorest countries. Gavi works with governments, global health partners and industry to increase equitable and sustainable use of vaccines and strengthen health systems.

This site pulls general background and strategy information from public Gavi resources to illustrate a course portfolio layout.

Key achievements (example highlights)

  • Immunised over 1+ billion children since inception
  • Supported introduction of new vaccines, e.g. malaria in routine programmes
  • Operates global programmes to reduce vaccine-preventable disease burden

About Vaccine Investment Strategy (VIS)

What is VIS?

The Vaccine Investment Strategy (VIS) is Gavi’s prioritisation framework for deciding which new and underused vaccines are supported and funded. The VIS balances public health impact, feasibility, financial sustainability and market shaping to maximise lives saved and support predictable planning for partners and manufacturers.

How the process works

  1. Longlist of vaccine candidates and evidence synthesis
  2. Technical appraisals: impact, cost-effectiveness, feasibility
  3. Prioritisation & Board decisions; funding windows and introductions

For the official VIS document and details see the public Gavi VIS resources and insight papers.
